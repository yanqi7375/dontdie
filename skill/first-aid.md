# First Aid Guidance

Provide relevant guidance based on the user's reported symptoms during an SOS. Keep instructions **short, clear, and actionable**. Always remind the user to call emergency services.

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer:** This is general first aid information, NOT medical advice. Always call your local emergency number (120/911/112) for professional help.

---

## Chest Pain / Heart Attack Symptoms

**Signs:** chest pain, pressure, tightness, pain radiating to arm/jaw/back, shortness of breath, nausea, cold sweat

**EN:**
1. Sit down or lie in a comfortable position — do NOT exert yourself
2. If you have aspirin and are not allergic, chew one regular aspirin (325mg)
3. Loosen any tight clothing
4. Try to stay calm and breathe slowly
5. Do NOT eat or drink anything else
6. Stay on the line — help is coming

**ZH:**
1. 坐下或躺在舒适的位置，不要用力活动
2. 如果有阿司匹林且不过敏，嚼服一片（325mg）
3. 松开紧身衣物
4. 保持冷静，慢慢呼吸
5. 不要吃喝其他东西
6. 保持联系，救援正在来

## Difficulty Breathing

**Signs:** can't breathe, shortness of breath, wheezing, gasping

**EN:**
1. Sit upright — do NOT lie flat
2. If you have an inhaler (for asthma), use it now
3. Loosen clothing around neck and chest
4. Breathe slowly: in through nose (4 sec), out through mouth (6 sec)
5. Open a window if possible for fresh air
6. Do NOT panic — controlled breathing helps

**ZH:**
1. 坐直身体，不要平躺
2. 如果有哮喘喷雾，现在就用
3. 松开脖子和胸部的衣物
4. 慢慢呼吸：鼻子吸气 4 秒，嘴巴呼气 6 秒
5. 如果可以，打开窗户通风
6. 不要恐慌，控制呼吸节奏

---

## Severe Bleeding

**Signs:** bleeding, cut, wound, blood

**EN:**
1. Apply firm, direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or clothing
2. Do NOT remove the cloth — add more layers on top if blood soaks through
3. If possible, elevate the injured area above heart level
4. Do NOT apply a tourniquet unless trained to do so
5. Stay still and wait for help

**ZH:**
1. 用干净的布或衣物用力直接按压伤口
2. 不要移开布，如果渗透了就在上面加更多层
3. 如果可以，把受伤部位抬高到心脏以上
4. 不要使用止血带，除非受过训练
5. 保持不动，等待救援

## Choking

**Signs:** choking, can't swallow, something stuck in throat

**EN:**
1. If you can cough, keep coughing forcefully
2. If alone: make a fist, place it above your belly button, grab with other hand, thrust upward firmly (self-Heimlich)
3. Or lean over a sturdy chair back and thrust your upper abdomen against it
4. Repeat until the object is expelled
5. If you cannot breathe at all, try to call emergency services immediately

**ZH:**
1. 如果能咳嗽，使劲咳
2. 如果一个人：握拳放在肚脐上方，另一手握住，用力向上冲击（自救海姆立克法）
3. 或者靠在椅背上，用上腹部撞击椅背
4. 反复进行直到异物排出
5. 如果完全无法呼吸，尽快拨打急救电话

## Allergic Reaction / Anaphylaxis

**Signs:** allergic reaction, swelling, hives, throat closing, can't breathe after eating/contact

**EN:**
1. If you have an EpiPen (epinephrine auto-injector), use it NOW — inject into outer thigh
2. Lie down with legs elevated (unless having trouble breathing — then sit up)
3. If you took the allergen orally, do NOT induce vomiting
4. Remove the source of allergen if possible
5. A second reaction can happen — stay alert

**ZH:**
1. 如果有肾上腺素笔（EpiPen），现在就用——注射在大腿外侧
2. 躺下并抬高双腿（如果呼吸困难就坐起来）
3. 如果是吃进去的过敏原，不要催吐
4. 尽可能远离过敏源
5. 可能出现二次反应，保持警觉

## Fall / Can't Move

**Signs:** I fell, I can't move, back pain after fall, I'm on the floor

**EN:**
1. Do NOT try to get up if you feel pain in your back, neck, or head
2. Stay as still as possible
3. If you can, keep your head and spine aligned
4. If you're cold, try to reach a blanket or clothing nearby
5. Keep talking to me — help is coming

**ZH:**
1. 如果背部、脖子或头部疼痛，不要试图起来
2. 尽量保持不动
3. 如果可以，保持头部和脊柱成一条直线
4. 如果冷，试着够到附近的毯子或衣物
5. 继续和我说话，救援正在来

---

## Seizure (Self-awareness)

**Signs:** seizure, convulsion, feeling a seizure coming

**EN:**
1. If you feel a seizure coming, lie down on the floor in a safe area
2. Move away from furniture, stairs, or sharp objects
3. Turn onto your side if possible (recovery position)
4. Do NOT put anything in your mouth
5. Try to note the time — seizures lasting over 5 minutes need immediate help

**ZH:**
1. 如果感觉癫痫要发作，躺到地上安全的地方
2. 远离家具、楼梯和尖锐物品
3. 如果可以，侧卧（恢复体位）
4. 不要往嘴里放任何东西
5. 尽量记住时间——超过 5 分钟的发作需要立即急救

## Panic Attack / Anxiety Attack

**Signs:** panic attack, heart racing, feel like dying, can't calm down, anxiety attack

**EN:**
1. You are safe. This feeling is temporary and will pass.
2. Breathe with me: in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 4
3. Focus on 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear
4. Place your feet flat on the ground — feel the solid surface
5. This is NOT a heart attack — but if you're unsure, it's always okay to call for help

**ZH:**
1. 你是安全的。这种感觉是暂时的，会过去的。
2. 跟我一起呼吸：吸气 4 秒，屏住 4 秒，呼气 4 秒
3. 看看周围 5 样能看到的东西，摸 4 样能摸到的，听 3 种声音
4. 双脚平放在地面上，感受地面的实感
5. 这不是心脏病发作——但如果不确定，叫救护车永远没错

## General (Unknown Emergency)

When the user sends SOS but doesn't describe symptoms:

**EN:**
> I've alerted your emergency contacts with your location. If you can, please call {emergency_number} directly — that's the fastest way to get help.
>
> Can you tell me what's happening? Even a word or two helps me provide better information to your contacts.
>
> I'm right here with you. 🛟

**ZH:**
> 已经把你的位置发给了紧急联系人。如果可以，请直接拨打 {emergency_number}——这是最快的求助方式。
>
> 能告诉我发生了什么吗？哪怕一两个词也能帮我给联系人提供更好的信息。
>
> 我一直在这里陪着你 🛟
